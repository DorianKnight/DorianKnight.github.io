McMaster University | September 2020 - April 2026 Major: Mechatronics Engineering
Minor: Biomedical Engineering

The Integrated Biomedical Engineering and Health Sciences (IBEHS) program fuses Engineering together with health sciences. This combination gives graduates a strong understanding of their Engineering discipline and how it relates to novel challenges in health care.

My journey through the IBEHS program has been within the Mechatronics engineering stream. In mechatronics we lean about how mechanical, electrical and software design come together to form intelligent control systems. Coupled together with the biomedical knowledge, IBio Mechatronics students have an intuitive understanding on the interface between man and machine. Everyday examples include continuous glucose monitors, responsive prosthetics and biomedical implants.

I hope to take my knowledge and connect the nervous system to computers so we can push the bounds of what is considered biologically possible when it comes to regaining lost mobility after a traumatic accident or pushing the bounds of what is within the reach of human cognition.
