Summary
I’m a mechatronics and biomedical engineering student studying at McMaster University, based in Hamilton, Ontario, Canada, set to graduate in April 2026. My interdisciplinary education has made me an asset when it comes to managing and working on projects that straddle many complex fields.
My Education
I study engineering through the Integrated Biomedical Engineering and Health Sciences program (IBioMed) at McMaster University. This program is the first of its kind in Canada and combines the study of engineering with health sciences.

Within the program, I have elected to pursue the “Mechatronics and Biomedical Engineering” specialization, which focuses on the intersection of smart robotics and modern healthcare challenges.

Upon completing my program, I will receive a B.Eng.BME (Bachelor of Engineering specializing in Biomedical Engineering).

What is Mechatronics Engineering?
Mechatronic engineers leverage their knowledge of software, computer systems and mechanical design to build smart adaptive systems ranging from surgical robots to self-driving cars.

Mechatronics undergraduate education gives students a foundational understanding of electrical, software and mechanical engineering disciplines.
What drives me?
The challenge of interfacing the nervous system with computers to treat disease and augment normal functioning.

Brain-computer interfaces, neuromorphic computing and surgical robotics are fields of great interest to me because of their potential to revolutionize the human condition.

Understanding neurodegenerative diseases/neuropathies like Alzheimer’s, dementia, ALS and Charcot-Marie-Tooth is of personal interest to me. Through my studies, I’ve been able to balance the engineering fundamentals with biological expertise to begin uncovering novel insights within this field.
